About

Decoding the Neoproterozoic–Cambrian Transition

I am a geologist and earth scientist with a PhD, specializing in Precambrian and early Paleozoic geology. My work centers on the late Ediacaran and early Cambrian sedimentary successions exposed along the southwestern margin of Baltica — the East European Platform.

Through sedimentological, geochemical, and stratigraphic analysis, I aim to reconstruct the paleoenvironmental and paleogeographic evolution of this ancient continental margin during one of the most transformative intervals in Earth history.

My research has been published in Precambrian Research and other Elsevier journals, contributing to our understanding of the Ediacaran–Cambrian boundary events and their global correlations.

Lithostratigraphy & Sedimentology

Detailed logging and facies analysis of the Vendian–Cambrian sedimentary succession, reconstructing depositional environments along the EEP passive margin.

Chemostratigraphy

Carbon and strontium isotope stratigraphy across the Ediacaran–Cambrian boundary, correlating excursions with global events and mass extinction horizons.

Paleoenvironmental Reconstruction

Integrating trace fossils, body fossils, and geochemical proxies to reconstruct the paleo-oceanographic conditions during the Cambrian Explosion.

Biostratigraphy & Correlation

Calibrating the Vendian–Cambrian succession of the EEP against global Ediacaran fossil assemblages and early skeletal fauna to constrain boundary ages.
